General Health Recommendations

Regardless of your risk level, maintaining a healthy lifestyle is crucial for preventing and managing diabetes. Here are some general guidelines:

Dietary Guidelines

  • Balanced Meals: Focus on whole foods — vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, whole grains.
  • Limit Sugars: Reduce sugary drinks, processed foods, refined carbohydrates.
  • Healthy Fats: Choose avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil.
  • Portion Control: Be mindful of portion sizes to manage calorie intake.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.

Lifestyle Adjustments

  • Stress Management: Practice meditation, yoga, or deep breathing.
  • Quit Smoking: If you smoke, quitting is one of the most impactful changes.
  • Limit Alcohol: Consume alcohol in moderation, if at all.
  • Regular Check-ups: Schedule regular health check-ups with your doctor.

Exercise Recommendations

  • Aerobic Activity: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week (brisk walking, cycling, swimming).
  • Strength Training: Incorporate strength exercises at least twice a week.
  • Stay Active: Reduce sedentary time with short walks or stretching breaks.

Sleep Hygiene

  • Consistent Schedule: Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily.
  • Adequate Sleep: Aim for 7–9 hours of quality sleep per night.
  • Limit Screens: Avoid screens at least an hour before bedtime.

Medical Considerations

  • Consult Your Doctor: Always discuss your risk assessment with a qualified medical professional.
  • Regular Monitoring: Your doctor may recommend A1C, fasting blood glucose tests.
  • Medication: Follow your doctor's advice regarding any prescribed medications.
